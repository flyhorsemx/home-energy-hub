Insulation upgrades can improve comfort, reduce heating and cooling load, and address attic air leaks. In 2026, incentive planning should start with current utility, state, and DOE-backed programs rather than old federal tax-credit assumptions.

## Utility and State Insulation Rebates (2026)

Many utilities offer direct cash rebates for insulation upgrades, with rules that vary by ZIP code, utility, income, and contractor participation:

| State/Utility | Rebate | Notes |
|--------------|--------|-------|
| Massachusetts (Mass Save) | Up to $2,000 | Among best in nation; full audit + rebate |
| Connecticut (Energize CT) | Up to $1,200 | Requires home energy assessment |
| New York (NYSERDA/utilities) | Up to $1,500 | Income-tiered |
| Maine (Efficiency Maine) | Up to $1,000 | Contractor must be Efficiency Maine listed |
| Vermont (Efficiency Vermont) | Up to $1,000 | Heat load analysis required |
| Wisconsin (Focus on Energy) | Up to $600 | ENERGY STAR contractor |
| Oregon (Energy Trust) | Up to $600 | Income-tiered |
| Michigan (DTE/Consumers) | Up to $400 | Home performance program |
| Colorado (Xcel) | Up to $400 | Xcel customers only |
| Maryland (BGE/Pepco) | Up to $400 | Maryland Smart Energy Communities |
| Pennsylvania (PPL/PECO) | Up to $400 | Utility-specific programs |

Most programs require a home energy audit before work begins. This audit (usually free through the utility program) identifies exactly where your home is losing energy and what upgrades will deliver the highest savings.

## Stacking Example: Connecticut Homeowner

| Incentive | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Attic insulation project cost | $3,200 |
| Energize CT rebate | -$800 |
| Net cost | $2,400 |
| Current tax eligibility | Verify before purchase |
| **Final cost** | **$1,680** |
| Annual energy savings | $320/yr |
| **Payback period** | **5.3 years** |

Without incentives: $3,200 cost ÷ $320/yr = 10-year payback. With incentives: $1,680 ÷ $320 = 5.3 years.

## Documentation to Keep Before You Claim Any Incentive

1. **Keep all documentation:** Contractor invoice, material receipts, and R-value specifications
2. **Verify eligibility:** Materials must be specifically installed to "improve the building envelope" (walls, floors, ceilings) — interior décor insulation doesn't qualify
3. **Verify current tax rules:** If you plan to claim any tax benefit, check current IRS instructions or ask a tax professional before relying on it
4. **Keep records 3 years:** Standard IRS audit window for home improvement credits

## The HEEHRA Low-Income Insulation Rebate

For households below 150% of area median income, the IRA's HEEHRA program provides additional rebates:
- Up to $1,600 for insulation and air sealing
- Administered by state energy offices
- Available as point-of-sale rebates in states that have launched the program

DOE, state, utility, and income-qualified programs may interact differently by state. Confirm current stacking rules before signing a contract.

Find a list of HEEHRA programs by state at your state's energy office website, or check the Department of Energy's IRA rebate tracker.
