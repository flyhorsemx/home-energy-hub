Insulation upgrades have one of the fastest payback periods of any home improvement — typically 3–7 years — and the 2025 incentive stack makes them even more attractive.

## Federal 25C Credit for Insulation

The IRA 25C credit applies to insulation and air sealing materials:
- **Credit:** 30% of the cost of qualifying insulation materials and installation
- **Annual maximum:** $1,200 (combined with other efficiency improvements like windows and HVAC)
- **Qualifying materials:** Fiberglass, cellulose, spray foam, mineral wool installed in walls, ceilings, floors, and attics
- **Applies to:** Primary residence; existing homes (not new construction)

**What's included:** The IRS guidance includes both materials AND installation labor for insulation. This is more generous than the windows credit, which also covers labor.

**Annual reset:** Like other 25C credits, this resets each year. A large insulation project can be spread across two years to capture $1,200 × 2 = $2,400 in total credits.

## Stacking Example: Connecticut Homeowner

| Incentive | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Attic insulation project cost | $3,200 |
| Energize CT rebate | -$800 |
| Net cost | $2,400 |
| IRA 25C credit (30% of $2,400) | -$720 |
| **Final cost** | **$1,680** |
| Annual energy savings | $320/yr |
| **Payback period** | **5.3 years** |

Without incentives: $3,200 cost ÷ $320/yr = 10-year payback. With incentives: $1,680 ÷ $320 = 5.3 years.

## How to Claim the 25C Insulation Credit

1. **Keep all documentation:** Contractor invoice, material receipts, and R-value specifications
2. **Verify eligibility:** Materials must be specifically installed to "improve the building envelope" (walls, floors, ceilings) — interior décor insulation doesn't qualify
3. **File Form 5695:** The credit is claimed under Part II of Form 5695 in the same category as windows and doors (the $1,200 combined annual cap applies)
4. **Keep records 3 years:** Standard IRS audit window for home improvement credits

## The HEEHRA Low-Income Insulation Rebate

For households below 150% of area median income, the IRA's HEEHRA program provides additional rebates:
- Up to $1,600 for insulation and air sealing
- Administered by state energy offices
- Available as point-of-sale rebates in states that have launched the program

HEEHRA and the 25C tax credit can be stacked, making insulation upgrades effectively free for many income-eligible households.

Find a list of HEEHRA programs by state at your state's energy office website, or check the Department of Energy's IRA rebate tracker.
