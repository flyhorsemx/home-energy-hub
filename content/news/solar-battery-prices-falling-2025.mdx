Home battery storage is reaching a cost inflection point. The installed cost of a single Tesla Powerwall 3 or Enphase IQ 5P has dropped roughly 20% since early 2024, and the IRA's 30% investment tax credit applies to battery-only installations (no solar required since August 2022).

## What's Driving Prices Down

**Chinese LFP cell prices:** Lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cell prices fell 40% between 2023 and 2025, driven by massive Chinese production expansion. Most US home battery manufacturers source LFP cells, though this is changing with domestic production incentives.

**IRA domestic manufacturing:** IRA manufacturing credits for US-made battery components are incentivizing domestic LFP production. More US manufacturing capacity is coming online in 2025–2026.

**Competition:** The home battery market has matured significantly. Tesla, Enphase, Franklin, Generac, Sungrow, and others now compete aggressively on price, benefiting consumers.

## When Does Battery Storage Make Financial Sense?

Battery storage makes the strongest financial case when at least one of these applies:

**1. You have solar + high export rates (California NEM 3.0)**
California's NEM 3.0 pays below retail for grid exports. Battery storage lets you use your own solar power instead of exporting at low rates and buying back at high rates. Payback: 6–9 years post-IRA credit in most CA markets.

**2. You have time-of-use rates with high peak pricing**
Many utilities now offer TOU rates where peak hours (4–9 PM) cost 3–5x off-peak rates. A battery charged on cheap off-peak power (or solar) can discharge during expensive peak hours. Annual savings of $600–$1,500 are common for well-utilized batteries.

**3. You need backup power resilience**
With increasing grid outages from storms and heat events, the value of backup power is real. A 13.5 kWh battery can power essential loads (refrigerator, lights, phone charging, medical equipment) for 12–24 hours during an outage.

**4. Your utility requires battery storage for NEM approval (some markets)**
Some utility jurisdictions are beginning to condition new solar interconnection approvals on battery storage inclusion.

## What Battery Storage Doesn't Do Well

Battery storage is not typically justified by energy savings alone in states with favorable net metering (Florida, Texas, Illinois, etc.). In these markets, the $7,000–$9,000 net cost after tax credit is difficult to justify on energy savings that may be $200–$400/year.

For most homeowners in net metering-friendly states, the decision is primarily about backup power value (how much is grid reliability worth to you?) rather than energy economics.

## The IRA Tax Credit for Battery Storage

The 30% Investment Tax Credit applies to:
- Standalone battery installations (no solar required since August 2022)
- Battery additions to existing solar systems
- New solar + battery systems

**Minimum capacity requirement:** 3 kWh (all major home batteries qualify)

**Claim on:** IRS Form 5695 in the year of installation

**No annual cap:** Unlike the 25C credit for HVAC and insulation, the residential clean energy credit (battery and solar) has no per-year dollar cap. If your battery installation costs $12,000, you receive a $3,600 credit.
