Your HVAC system is the most expensive appliance in your home. Proper maintenance can extend its life from 12 years to 18+ years and cut energy bills by up to 15%. Here's the definitive homeowner checklist.

## ✅ Safe for DIY: Monthly & Seasonal Tasks

### 1. Replace the Air Filter (Every 1–3 Months)

This is the single most impactful thing you can do. A clogged filter forces your system to work harder, increasing energy use and wear on the blower motor.

**How to do it:**
1. Locate your return air vent (usually a large grille on a wall or ceiling)
2. Note the filter size printed on the frame (e.g., 20x25x1)
3. Slide out the old filter, note the airflow direction arrow
4. Insert the new filter with the arrow pointing toward the blower

**Recommended filters:** MERV 8–11 for most homes. MERV 13+ can restrict airflow in older systems.

### 2. Clear the Condensate Drain Line (Every 3 Months)

A clogged drain line causes water overflow and can trigger system shutoff.

**How to do it:**
- Locate the PVC drain pipe near your indoor unit
- Pour 1/4 cup of distilled white vinegar into the access port
- Follow with a cup of water after 30 minutes

### 3. Clean the Outdoor Condenser (Twice a Year)

Turn off power at the disconnect box first. Gently rinse fins with a garden hose (low pressure, inside-out). Remove leaves and debris from around the unit.

### 4. Check Thermostat Batteries and Settings

Replace batteries annually. Verify your thermostat schedule is optimized for seasonal changes — a properly programmed smart thermostat (like Google Nest or Ecobee) can save an additional $50–$100/year.

## ⚠️ Requires a Licensed HVAC Technician

### Refrigerant Check and Recharge

Low refrigerant ("Freon") is a symptom of a leak — not a maintenance item. Only EPA 608-certified technicians can legally purchase, handle, or recharge refrigerants. If your system is blowing warm air, this requires a pro.

### Electrical Component Inspection

Capacitors, contactors, and control boards handle dangerous voltages (up to 440V). Annual professional inspections check for:
- Failing capacitors (causes hard starts and compressor damage)
- Loose connections (fire hazard)
- Refrigerant line insulation integrity

### Duct Sealing and Balancing

Leaky ducts waste 20–30% of conditioned air. Sealing requires mastic sealant or metal tape applied inside ducts — a job requiring crawlspace access and professional diagnostic tools.

### Coil Cleaning (Evaporator)

The indoor evaporator coil requires chemical cleaning every 2–3 years. Improper cleaning damages delicate aluminum fins and can cause refrigerant leaks.

## Annual Professional Maintenance: What It Includes

A proper annual tune-up ($80–$150) by a licensed HVAC technician covers:
- Refrigerant level check and leak detection
- Electrical component testing (capacitors, contactors)
- Blower motor lubrication and amp draw
- Evaporator and condenser coil inspection
- Full system efficiency test

**Pro tip:** Schedule in early spring (before cooling season) or early fall. Technicians are in higher demand — and charge more — during summer heatwaves and winter cold snaps.
