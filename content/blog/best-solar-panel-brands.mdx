## Brand-by-Brand Deep Dive

### 1. SunPower (Now Maxeon Solar Technologies)

SunPower's residential panels are manufactured by Maxeon Solar Technologies, a spin-off that continues to produce the industry's most efficient mass-market panels. The flagship Maxeon 6 series reaches **22.8% efficiency** — about 30% more output per square foot than a budget panel.

**What makes them stand out:**
- Copper foundation cell design that resists cracking and corrosion
- Industry-leading 40-year warranty (product and performance combined)
- Lowest degradation rate in the industry (~0.25% per year vs. 0.5–0.8% industry average)
- Backed by Maxeon's global manufacturing and service network

**Who should buy SunPower:** Homeowners with limited roof space who need maximum output, or those planning to own the home long-term and want the most durable option available.

**Drawbacks:** Premium pricing — expect to pay $0.50–$1.00/watt more than comparable brands. SunPower's dealer network is narrower than Qcells or Silfab.

### 2. Panasonic EverVolt

Panasonic's HIT (Heterojunction) technology panels have long been a favorite among quality-conscious installers. The EverVolt H Series reaches **22.2% efficiency** with excellent temperature coefficient performance — meaning they hold up better on hot days than standard monocrystalline panels.

**What makes them stand out:**
- Superior performance in high-heat climates (low temperature coefficient of -0.26%/°C)
- 25-year comprehensive warranty with strong degradation guarantee (90.76% at year 25)
- Panasonic's brand stability and global manufacturing history
- Available in all-black aesthetic for roofline-conscious homeowners

**Who should buy Panasonic:** Homeowners in hot climates (Southwest, Southeast) where panel temperature affects performance, and those who want a balance of premium quality and slightly lower cost than SunPower.

**Drawbacks:** Pricing is still at the premium end. Panasonic exited North American panel manufacturing and now relies on contract manufacturing — verify your installer's supply chain.

### 3. REC Group (Alpha Pure-R)

REC is a Norwegian manufacturer with a strong reputation in Europe that has been gaining ground in the US market. Their Alpha Pure-R series achieves **22.3% efficiency** using heterojunction technology similar to Panasonic — at a slightly more competitive price point.

**What makes them stand out:**
- 25-year product and performance warranty (92% output guaranteed at year 25 — best in class at this price)
- Split Cell technology reduces energy loss from partial shading
- Strong independent quality testing scores (PV Evolution Labs Top Performer)
- Made in Singapore with rigorous quality control

**Who should buy REC:** Value-seekers who don't want to compromise on quality or warranty terms. REC offers near-SunPower performance guarantees at $0.20–$0.50/watt less.

**Drawbacks:** Brand recognition in the US is lower than SunPower or Panasonic, which can affect resale narrative. Installer availability in some rural areas is limited.

### 4. Qcells (Q.PEAK DUO BLK Series)

Qcells is a South Korean manufacturer (now owned by Hanwha) that has become the volume leader in the US residential market. Their Q.PEAK DUO BLK panels are the most commonly installed residential panels in America — a testament to their installer relationships, supply reliability, and price.

**What makes them stand out:**
- Manufactured in Dalton, Georgia (Buy America provisions may apply for certain incentive programs)
- Competitive efficiency (21.4%) at a meaningful price discount vs. premium brands
- Anti-LID and Anti-PID technology for longevity
- Widely available through major installers nationwide
- 25-year product and performance warranty

**Who should buy Qcells:** Budget-conscious homeowners with adequate roof space, or those prioritizing installer availability and proven track record over maximum efficiency.

**Drawbacks:** Performance warranty floor (86% at year 25) is lower than SunPower, Panasonic, or REC — meaning slightly higher long-term degradation.

### 5. Silfab (SIL-380 BK)

Silfab is a Canadian manufacturer with US facilities in Bellingham, Washington. They've carved a niche as a quality US-manufactured alternative for homeowners who prioritize domestic production and solid (if not class-leading) specs.

**What makes them stand out:**
- Manufactured in North America (Canada and Washington State)
- 30-year product warranty — one of the longest in the industry
- Competitive pricing at Qcells-comparable rates
- Good PTC ratings for real-world performance

**Who should buy Silfab:** Homeowners who want a North American-made panel with a longer-than-standard product warranty at a mid-range price.

**Drawbacks:** Efficiency (20.9%) and performance warranty floor (80.2% at year 25) are below premium brands. Smaller service network if issues arise.

## How to Choose the Right Brand for Your Home

### Prioritize Efficiency If:
- Your roof space is limited (townhouse, smaller home)
- You want maximum long-term production
- Budget allows for premium panels

### Prioritize Value If:
- You have ample south-facing roof space
- You're budget-conscious and comparing financing options
- Your installer has strong relationships with Qcells or Silfab

### Key Questions to Ask Your Installer

1. Which brands do you carry, and are you a certified installer for that brand?
2. What degradation rate is guaranteed in writing on the performance warranty?
3. Who handles warranty claims — you or the manufacturer?
4. Can I see a production estimate comparing different panel options?

## Brands to Approach with Caution

The solar panel market also includes dozens of Tier 2 and Tier 3 manufacturers offering very low prices. Brands without a 25-year US-backed warranty, no track record of US warranty claims, or that have changed ownership multiple times are risks — because a warranty is only as good as the company standing behind it.

LG Solar is worth noting: LG exited the solar panel manufacturing business in 2022. If you see LG panels offered, they are existing inventory — panels are still quality, but no new warranty claims can be made against LG Solar as an ongoing entity. Verify warranty backing carefully.

## Conclusion

In 2025, SunPower (Maxeon) remains the clear leader on efficiency and warranty terms — but at a price premium that won't suit every budget. Panasonic and REC offer compelling alternatives with near-premium performance at lower cost. Qcells is the smart choice for homeowners who want proven quality, wide installer availability, and competitive pricing. Silfab earns its place for buyers who value North American manufacturing and a long product warranty.
