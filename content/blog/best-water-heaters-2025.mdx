## Best Heat Pump Water Heater: Rheem ProTerra 50-Gallon

**Price:** ~$1,100–$1,400 | **UEF:** 3.75 | **First Hour Rating:** 66 gal

The Rheem ProTerra is the most efficient mainstream HPWH available, with a market-leading UEF of 3.75 — meaning it delivers $3.75 worth of hot water for every $1 of electricity. Wi-Fi connectivity lets you monitor energy use and set schedules from your phone.

**Pros:**
- Highest efficiency in its class
- EcoNet Wi-Fi app is well-designed
- Qualifies for full 30% IRA tax credit
- 10-year warranty on tank and parts

**Cons:**
- Needs 700+ cubic feet of air space
- ~50 dB noise (like a running dishwasher)
- 240V/30A required

**Best for:** Replacing an electric resistance tank in a basement or garage. With the 30% tax credit, payback is often 2–3 years vs. standard electric.

## Best Gas Tank Water Heater: A.O. Smith Signature Plus 50-Gallon

**Price:** ~$650–$850 | **UEF:** 0.67 | **First Hour Rating:** 89 gal

A.O. Smith consistently scores highest in reliability surveys, and the Signature Plus is the sweet spot of their gas tank line. The 89-gallon first hour rating handles large households without running out. 9-year warranty is above-average for a tank at this price.

**Pros:**
- Best-in-class reliability reputation
- High recovery rate (87 gallons/hour)
- Available at major retailers
- Brass drain valve (not plastic)

**Cons:**
- 0.67 UEF is average, not exceptional
- Slightly more expensive than entry-level competitors

**Runner-Up: Bradford White RG250T6N**
Bradford White water heaters are sold through plumbing supply houses (not big-box stores), which means fresher inventory and arguably better manufacturing oversight. Many plumbers prefer them. Slightly harder to purchase independently.

## Best Gas Tankless: Navien NPE-240A2

**Price:** ~$1,000–$1,400 (unit only) | **UEF:** 0.97 | **Flow Rate:** 11.2 GPM

The Navien NPE-240A2 is one of the few tankless units with a built-in recirculation pump — meaning you get hot water at the tap in seconds instead of waiting 10–20 seconds for cold water to purge. Combined with a 0.97 UEF and 11.2 GPM flow rate, it's the best all-around gas tankless for most American homes.

**Pros:**
- Built-in recirculation pump (near-instant hot water)
- 0.97 UEF qualifies for federal tax credit
- 11.2 GPM handles large families easily
- NaviLink Wi-Fi interface

**Cons:**
- Higher unit cost than basic Rinnai models
- Installation requires professional (complex commissioning)
- 3/4" gas line strongly recommended

**Runner-Up: Rinnai RU Series**
Rinnai is the market leader in gas tankless with excellent reliability data. The RU Series (RU130iN for 7.5 GPM, RU199iN for 11 GPM) is a solid alternative if your installer has more Rinnai experience.

## How to Size Your Water Heater

### By Household Size

| Household | Recommended Tank Size | Recommended Tankless Flow |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 people | 30–40 gallons | 5–7 GPM |
| 2–3 people | 40–50 gallons | 6–8 GPM |
| 3–4 people | 50 gallons | 7–9 GPM |
| 4–5 people | 50–80 gallons | 9–11 GPM |
| 5+ people | 80 gallons or tankless | 10–11+ GPM |

### By First Hour Rating (FHR)

First Hour Rating matters more than tank size — it tells you how much hot water the unit can deliver in the first hour of heavy use. For a family of 4, look for FHR of 70–90 gallons.
