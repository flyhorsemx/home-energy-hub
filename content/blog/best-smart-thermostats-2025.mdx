## Smart Thermostat Compatibility Guide

Before purchasing, verify compatibility with your system:

| HVAC Type | Compatible Thermostats |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Gas furnace + central AC | All models listed |
| Heat pump (single stage) | All models listed |
| Heat pump with aux heat | Ecobee (best), Nest Learning, Honeywell T9 |
| Electric baseboard (high voltage) | **None** — these are 120/240V systems |
| Boiler/radiant heat | Ecobee (with 24V zone valve), check others |
| Multi-stage heating/cooling | Ecobee, Nest Learning |

**Pro tip:** Check your existing thermostat wiring. Take a photo before disconnecting. The presence of a C-wire (blue wire on most systems) determines which thermostats you can install without an adapter.

## Do Smart Thermostats Qualify for Tax Credits?

Smart thermostats themselves do not qualify for federal tax credits. However, they are often bundled with qualifying HVAC equipment upgrades. Some states (including Connecticut, Oregon, and Massachusetts) offer direct smart thermostat rebates through utility programs.

**Utility rebates:** Many utilities pay $50–$100 for smart thermostat installation through demand response programs. Check your utility's website or DSIRE (dsireusa.org) for current offers.

## Installation: DIY or Hire a Pro?

Smart thermostat installation is a popular DIY project. Most installations take 30–60 minutes:

1. Turn off power at the breaker
2. Remove old thermostat and photograph wiring
3. Mount new thermostat base and reconnect wires (labeled slots)
4. Attach thermostat, restore power, and follow app setup

**When to hire a pro:** If your system uses a heat pump with complex wiring (O/B wire, auxiliary heat, dual-fuel), or if you have a proprietary system, professional installation ($75–$150) ensures proper configuration.
